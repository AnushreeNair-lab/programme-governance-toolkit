## 📌 Purpose

This RACI Matrix defines who is **Responsible**, **Accountable**, **Consulted**, and **Informed** for every key governance activity across the programme. It eliminates ambiguity, prevents duplication of effort, and ensures the right people are involved at the right time.

Use this at programme kick-off and revisit it whenever the team structure changes.

## 📖 RACI Definitions

| Letter | Role | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **R** | Responsible | Does the work — directly carries out the activity |
| **A** | Accountable | Owns the outcome — signs off and is answerable for the result. Only ONE per activity |
| **C** | Consulted | Provides input — two-way communication, consulted before decisions are made |
| **I** | Informed | Kept updated — one-way communication, notified of decisions and progress |

### Golden Rules
- ✅ Every activity must have **exactly one A** (Accountable)
- ✅ Every activity must have **at least one R** (Responsible)
- ⚠️ Too many C's slow things down — consult only those whose input genuinely changes the outcome
- ⚠️ Too many I's create noise — inform only those who truly need to know

## 📋 How to Adapt This Matrix

### Step 1 — Confirm your roles
Replace the column headers with the actual roles or names on your programme. Remove any roles that don't apply and add any that are missing.

### Step 2 — Review each activity
Go through each row and confirm the RACI assignments are correct for your context. Challenge any row that has more than one **A** or no **R**.

### Step 3 — Validate with stakeholders
Share the matrix with each role holder and confirm they understand and accept their responsibilities. Do this at kick-off.

### Step 4 — Version and maintain
Update the matrix version number and date whenever roles change or new activities are added. Keep it as a living document throughout the programme.

## ⚠️ Common RACI Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why it's a problem | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Multiple Accountable owners | No clear decision-maker — leads to confusion and delays | Assign exactly one A per activity |
| R and A on the same person for everything | Single point of failure — no checks and balances | Distribute across roles |
| Too many Consulted | Decision-making slows down — too many opinions | Only consult those whose input changes the outcome |
| No one Informed | Stakeholders surprised by decisions | Ensure impacted parties are always on the I list |
| Matrix never updated | Becomes irrelevant as programme evolves | Review at every major milestone or team change |
